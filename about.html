ABOUT HEART DISEASE

Heart disease is a primary cause of death across the world. The World Health Organization (WHO) reports that cardiovascular diseases (CVDs) are responsible for 17.9 million deaths each year, which makes up 32% of all global fatalities. A significant number of these deaths, over 75%, happen in low- and middle-income countries. This creates serious health and economic problems for these nations.

The State of Heart Disease in Pakistan

The situation in Pakistan is particularly concerning. According to the 2019 Global Burden of Disease study, the death rate from cardiovascular disease in Pakistan was 357.88 for every 100,000 people. This rate is substantially higher than the global average of 239.85. This high rate is linked to widespread risk factors in the population, such as high blood pressure, diabetes, smoking, and obesity. These factors mean that a large portion of the population is at risk of developing heart disease.

Limitations of Conventional Diagnostic Methods

Currently, doctors predict heart disease using methods like physical examinations and diagnostic tests such as electrocardiograms (ECGs). While these methods are useful, they have limitations. They can be expensive, may require invasive procedures, and are often unavailable in many parts of the country. As a result, many individuals are not identified as high-risk until after they suffer a serious heart-related event, such as a heart attack.

Machine Learning for Improved Prediction

This study explores the use of machine learning to improve heart disease prediction. Machine learning uses computer algorithms to analyze health data and find hidden patterns. By examining patient information like age, blood pressure, cholesterol levels, and other risk factors, a machine learning model can calculate a person's risk of developing heart disease. This approach is non-invasive and can be more cost-effective than traditional methods. The purpose of this research is to develop a reliable machine learning model that can predict the risk of heart disease based on common health data. The goal is to create a tool that can help doctors identify at-risk patients early. Early identification allows for timely intervention through lifestyle changes and treatment, which can help prevent serious illness and reduce the number of deaths from heart disease in Pakistan.



CAD is the most common heart problem and the biggest killer

With CAD, you get blockages in your coronary arteries — the vessels that supply blood to your heart. That can reduce the flow of blood to your heart muscle, keeping it from getting the oxygen it needs. The disease usually starts as a result of atherosclerosis, sometimes called hardening of the arteries. When you have that condition, deposits called plaque build up in your arteries, often over many years.

Coronary heart disease can give you pain in your chest, called angina, or lead to a heart attack. It can also contribute to other heart problems, such as abnormal heart rhythms or heart failure.


Some things that may put you at a higher risk of CAD are:
  • Age (For men, the risk of heart disease goes up after age 55; for women, the risk rises sharply after menopause.)
  • Being inactive
  • Having diabetes or metabolic syndrome
  • Family history of coronary heart disease
  • Genetics
  • High blood pressure
  • High levels of LDL "bad" cholesterol or low levels of HDL "good" cholesterol
  • Obesity
  • Smoking
  • Stress
