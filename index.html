Heart Disease

Heart disease represents a complex and multifaceted array of conditions that profoundly impact the structure and function of the heart. Far from being a singular ailment, this umbrella term encompasses a diverse spectrum of pathologies, including diseases affecting blood vessels such as coronary artery disease (CAD), irregularities in heart rhythm known as arrhythmias, and structural cardiac abnormalities present from birth, referred to as congenital heart defects.

Protect Your Heart

Eat a Heart-Healthy Diet

Eating foods that are low in unhealthy fats, sugar, and salt helps keep your blood vessels clear and your heart strong. Focus on whole, natural foods instead of packaged or fried items.

Be Active Every Day

Moving your body helps your heart pump blood more efficiently, lowers your blood pressure, and improves cholesterol levels. You don’t need a gym—simple activities count!

Stop Smoking and Alcohol

Smoking damages the lining of blood vessels and makes your heart work harder. Alcohol in large amounts can raise blood pressure and add extra calories.

Manage Stress

Regular check-ups help you find problems (like high blood pressure or high cholesterol) early, when they are easier to treat. Managing stress keeps hormones in balance and prevents your heart from working too hard.
